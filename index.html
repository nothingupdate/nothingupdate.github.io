How to Manually Sideload OTA Updates

If you prefer not to wait for the staggered OTA (Over-The-Air) rollout to reach your specific region, you can manually sideload the official update ZIP files using Nothing's built-in offline update tool. Follow these steps carefully:

  • Download the Firmware: Find the correct incremental or full OTA ZIP file for your specific device model from our live feed above. Ensure the package region matches your device (Global, EEA, or India).
  • Prepare the Storage: Connect your phone to a PC or use a file manager app to create a new folder named ota (all lowercase) in the root directory of your internal storage.
  • Transfer the File: Copy the downloaded ZIP file into the ota folder.
  • Access the Hidden Tool: Open the default Phone dialer app and enter the secret code *#*#682#*#*. This triggers the offline update utility interface.
  • Install & Reboot: The utility will automatically scan the ota folder. Select the package and tap 'Directly apply OTA from selection'. Do not restart your phone manually; wait until the process says 'Successful' and prompts you to reboot.

Safety Guidelines & Best Practices

Modifying system software comes with inherent risks. To ensure a smooth installation process, always verify that your battery is charged above 50% before initiating any update. It is highly recommended to perform a comprehensive backup of your internal storage. If you are upgrading from a stable build to a Beta Hub release, expect potential bugs and increased battery drain while the system optimizes.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do you post updates for CMF devices too?

Yes, we provide instant firmware updates and app changelogs for the entire CMF ecosystem, including the CMF Phone 1, Watch Pro, and audio devices.

Will sideloading void my Nothing Phone warranty?

No. Using the official offline update tool uses software officially cryptographically signed by Nothing Technology. It does not unlock your bootloader, require root access, or void your hardware warranty.

Why is my update failing to install?

Installation failures are typically caused by corrupted downloads, insufficient storage, or renaming the file incorrectly (e.g., update.zip.zip). Delete the file, redownload it on a stable connection, and try again.
